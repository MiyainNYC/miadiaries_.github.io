Some Cool Projects I Work On

My past projects include applying probabilistic models to solve various graph mining problems and conducting exhaustive exloratory analysis for business optimization. Here below are those I find particularly interesting:

Deep learning demands large computation power and fine parameter tuning which consume massive time and effort. This project demonstrates the facility and effectiveness of transfer learning and ensemble. With single laptop, tiny data size and little preprocessing effort, the model can already reach beat-market accuracy in tackling problem of image classification .
Estimating employees performance is an important consideration in all organizations. This project proposes a network analysis approach to the estimation and visualization of employee effort, productivity and collaboration patterns. Questions we attempt to answer include identification of developersâ€™ role, functionality, motivation and working preference, as well as in a broader sense, corporate cohesion, collaboration patterns and business productivity. We demonstrate how a network analysis approach can help companies gain rich insights about these questions.
Graph clustering and community detection reveal insights regarding network structure, behavioral patterns of nodes and their relations. This project, by embracing techniques of community detection, clustering and customized graph projection as similarity functions, manages to build a effective collaborative filtering system which is aimed at optimizing workload assignment in software development environment. The graph based 2LH algorithm we introduced reaches high performance acroos a variety of common and customized metrics and has been proved as robust, flexible and sustainable.

Mobile app development have turned into an enormously profitable business. Profits however are not distributed equally amongst developers: 47% of developers make less than $100 and more than half of which make nothing at all. In this project, we propose an ensemble model which combines multiple ML algorithms, namely Naive Bayes, Extra Trees and Gaussian Process with different kernels, to predict app success through analyzing text info (description, customer reviews, etc.) of massive Android game apps.
Traditional cab hailing business has faced fierce challenges from Uber, Lyft and other high tech cab-hailing business. With a large dynamic network data of NYC green cab ridership, this project delivers deep-dive insights about the up-to-date industry overview. We help optimize cab drivers' profitability through an exhaustive exploratory analyis including time series analysis and graph analysis. We also propose a graph model combining random walk and pagerank to help drivers discover time-sensitive pickup opportunities.
